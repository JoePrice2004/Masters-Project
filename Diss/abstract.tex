This thesis investigates the analytical structure of complex integration along with its applications to two principal areas, those of which being the inversion of the Laplace transform via contour integration, and the asymptotic approximation of complex integrals via the method of steepest descent. The work is developed from first principles, beginning with the foundational results of complex analysis before moving towards the more advanced techniques that form the core of the thesis.

The inverse Laplace transform is treated in full generality, covering the standard pole case, the modification required when branch points are present, and the case of infinitely many singularities. The method of steepest descent is developed in its fully general form and then applied to general problems. Throughout, the emphasis is on understanding both the mechanics of each method and the underlying complex analysis that justifies it, rather than treating these techniques as computational tools alone or relying on a table of predetermined results.
